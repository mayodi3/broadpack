About Kakamega Forest Reserve

Kakamega Forest Reserve stands as Kenya's last remaining tropical rainforest, a precious ecosystem that has survived for millions of years. This 238-square-kilometer forest is a biodiversity hotspot, home to over 400 bird species, 400 butterfly species, and numerous mammals including the rare De Brazza's monkey, giant forest hog, and various antelope species. The forest offers an incredible canopy walk experience, allowing visitors to walk among the treetops and observe wildlife from a unique perspective. The reserve also holds significant cultural importance to the local Luhya community, with sacred sites and traditional medicine plants scattered throughout.
